Ravitoto

What is this?

Ravitoto is a traditional Malagasy cuisine. It means “crushed cassava leaves”. These are specifically sweet cassava (cassava tree) leaves pounded with a mortar or meat grinder. It is cooked with garlic and very fatty pork.

Fun Fact: It is a favourite dish of my boyfriend Mitsu.

Ingredients:

  • 800 g of the fattest pork belly
  • 500 g of crushed cassava leaves
  • 200 ml of sunflower oil
  • 4 cloves of garlic
  • 1 teaspoon of ginger
  • 1 onion or shallot

How to do it?

  1. Cut the meat into large cubes (my boyfriend said bigger = better), then sauté it in oil until golden brown.
  2. In a pot, brown the sliced shallot in a little oil, then add the garlic and cassava leaves. Mix well and add the ginger (just to perfume) and the oil.
  3. Cook over low heat for about 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. It's ready when the cassava leaves have completely changed color (from green to almost black).

And VOILA! EASY PEASY!
