Rosół (Chicken Soup)

What is this?

Rosół is a traditional Polish chicken soup made by simmering chicken, vegetables (such as carrots, celery, and onions), and spices (like peppercorns, bay leaves, and parsley) in water. The resulting broth is clear and flavorful, often served with fine egg noodles or homemade pasta. It’s a beloved comfort food in Poland, commonly enjoyed on Sundays and during special occasions..

Fun Fact: My favourite dish in the whole word

Ingredients:

  • 2 kg of chicken tighs, wings or whole body with neck
  • optional: 300 g of beef preferebly with bones
  • optional: 300 g of chicken stomachs, hearts etc
  • 3 carrots
  • 1 parsley
  • 1 onion
  • 1 celery
  • 1 leek
  • bay leaf
  • salt and pepper

How to do it?

  1. If we have a whole chicken, it should be cut into parts - cut off the neck, wings, thighs. Leave the body together with the breasts.
  2. Rinse all parts of chicken and beef and offal, then put in a large pot and pour cold water. Salt and boil over medium heat.
  3. After boiling, reduce the fire and hum the decoction. Reduce the heat and cook over low heat (the broth is only supposed to "blink") under a slightly ajar lid for about 1 and 1/2 hours.
  4. In the meantime, prepare vegetables: peel carrots, parsley and celery (or for a larger aroma - just wash thoroughly). Rinse the onion, leek and parsley (do not peel the onion, its husks will give the soup a nice color).
  5. Put the vegetables in the broth and boil. Add spices: allspice, bay leaf and black pepper. Reduce the heat and cook over low heat for about 1 hour or up to 1 and 1/2 hour.
  6. Serve the broth hot, with cooked pasta, chopped parsley, thinly sliced carrots from the broth and pieces of chicken meat.
